Puttymo

Given that over forty and a one months time have passed since the Chalk Farm fiasco, I have recently felt the compulsion and possibly the desire, and maybe even the urge if you will, to look into maybe taking up some new form of semi-employment.
As those of you know me well will know quite well or even weller, I am a man who likes to keep busy, and must at all times be using my hands for constructive purposes. Bad things have happened when I have become distracted or bored. For example I once knitted a badger to its own mind over a particularly quiet bank holiday weekend.
I have spent over twoty or threety years and thirty emblems at reed; it was there I first learnt to communicate solely with my eyebrows, how to make socks from road kill, and how to become employed through a care in the community program without anyone actually finding out about my uni-bottocked situation or my brain spoon.
For these reasons I have become quite attached to the place and thus it was quite a difficult decision to actually leave. I felt like a very handsome blister being suddenly ripped from its host.

Farming chalk

I was offered a position, namely that of chalk farmer. Chalk supplies in London are very low at the moment, due to a lack of natural predators, apparently (I was confused at this point). I accepted immediately.
Interestingly it was at a small to medium sounding farm in the Chalk Farm area. I had always wanted to farm chalk at a chalk farm in chalk farm. But I was very concerned that should anyone ask me what I did for a living it would sound faintly ridiculous. So I killed myself and set fire to the body fourteen times, to make sure no one found the evidence.
I didn't apologise the recruiterment consulterant so I will probably remain in limbo for eternity like Universal Healthcare.

Advert

Can you test that all of our custard is thick enough to meet our high standards?

By that I mean you should not be able to swim in it even if you are really really strong, like a carbon fiber whiskey.

When I was a boy, I often dreamt of working in a custard factory. Imagine, I thought to myself during one of my wetter childish reveries, imagine if I could actually walk about in a place that makes my favourite yellow non-newtonian liquid-based dessert. I would probably either die of happy, or just be quite pleased for a while until I realised it was a fruitless* dream and then moved on to the trifle factory down the road. (ha)

Either way, Imagine! I reveried. I reveried hard. Harder than anyone else of that era, to my knowledge.
Today, that dream has come true. And even though you should never start a sentence with And, I want to share that never ending joy with you, an unemployed custard-ignoring fiend.

Imagine!
